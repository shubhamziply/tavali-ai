## Key takeaways

- Denials are rising because of stricter payer rules, more complex coding, and workflows that catch errors only after submission.
- Most denials are preventable — they come from missing documentation, eligibility gaps, and coding mismatches.
- Checking denial risk *before* a claim is submitted is the single highest-leverage change a practice can make.

Claim denials are quietly one of the largest controllable drains on a dental practice’s revenue. A denied claim isn’t just delayed money — it’s staff time to rework, appeal, and resubmit, and a meaningful share that never gets collected at all.

## Why are denials going up?

Three forces are compounding at once:

- **Stricter payer adjudication.** Payers are automating more of their review and rejecting claims that would have slipped through a few years ago.
- **More complex coding.** The gap between the code a practice habitually uses and the most accurate code keeps widening.
- **After-the-fact workflows.** Most practices only discover a problem once the claim bounces back — days or weeks later.

## Where the revenue actually leaks

The leak rarely comes from one dramatic failure. It’s an accumulation of small, preventable misses:

- Eligibility wasn’t verified, so coverage assumptions were wrong.
- Documentation didn’t support the procedure billed.
- The wrong code — or an out-of-date one — was selected out of habit.
- The claim was clean, but follow-up on the denial never happened.

## How to stop it

The highest-leverage fix is to move the check **before** submission. Score each claim for denial risk while it can still be corrected, ground coding in authoritative code sets rather than habit, and carry documentation forward automatically so the evidence is already attached. When the whole workflow is connected — finding to chart to claim — the gaps where denials hide simply close.

That’s the difference between chasing denials after the fact and preventing them by design.
