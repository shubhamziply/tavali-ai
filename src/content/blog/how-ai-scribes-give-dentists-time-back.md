## Key takeaways

- An AI scribe listens to the visit and drafts the clinical note, so the provider reviews and approves instead of typing from scratch.
- It can cut documentation time substantially while keeping the clinician in control of the final record.
- The biggest wins come when the scribe is part of a connected system, not a standalone add-on.

Documentation is one of the least-loved parts of clinical work — and one of the biggest time sinks. Ambient **AI scribes** aim to give that time back by drafting the note as the visit happens.

## What an AI scribe does

A dental AI scribe captures the encounter and turns it into a structured draft: findings, procedures, and the clinical narrative, formatted the way the practice documents. The provider reviews, edits if needed, and approves. The work shifts from *writing* to *verifying*.

## What an AI scribe doesn’t do

It doesn’t make clinical decisions, and it doesn’t finalize anything on its own. The provider remains the author of record. A good scribe is explicit about uncertainty and never silently fills gaps — it surfaces what it heard and lets the clinician confirm.

## Why integration matters

A scribe that only produces a block of text leaves most of the value on the table. When the note is part of a connected operating system, the same captured finding can flow into the treatment plan, the estimate, and ultimately a cleaner claim — no re-keying, no lost context.

That’s the real promise: not just faster notes, but documentation that does double duty across the whole workflow.
